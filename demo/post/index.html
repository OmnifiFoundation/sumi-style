In recent years data sovereignty has been gaining momentum as a foundational driver for various digital projects. If you missed our [introduction into data sovereignty](../introduction-to-data-sovereignty), we'd highly recommend you take the opportunity to read it before going further. We'll not explain data sovereignty in this article.

Predominently the projects fall into one of two categories:

  • Distributed data storage, often backed by varying approaches to zero-knowledge proofs, and;
  • Politically-driven programs to develop regional competitive data markets to reduce the dependency on US tech companies.

Although a lot of conversation has emerged from projects in both of these camps, those conversations rarely discuss human needs when it comes to data sovereignty.
